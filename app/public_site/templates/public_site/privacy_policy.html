Privacy at Contractorz

YOUR WORKFLOW. YOUR INFORMATION. HANDLED WITH CARE.

This policy explains how Contractorz handles personal information when people visit our public pages, contact us, create an account, or use the platform to manage service work.

1. Scope and privacy roles

This policy applies to the Contractorz website, marketplace, account experience, and business-management platform.

Contractorz manages account, platform, contact, security, and payment-integration information for operating the service. A service business using Contractorz also decides why and how it collects information about its clients, employees, properties, and jobs. That business is responsible for using the platform lawfully, giving any notices it is required to give, and responding to requests concerning information it controls.

Canadian private-sector privacy laws, including Alberta's Personal Information Protection Act and the federal Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act, may apply depending on the organization, activity, and location.

2. Information we collect

Account and profile information

Name, email address, phone number, password credential, account role, profile photo, account status, verification state, and last-login information.

Business and team information

Business name and number, contact details, description, logo, service categories, address, timezone, team membership, job duties, and expertise.

Client, service, and property information

Client contact details; service type, scope, price, currency, billing cycle, and status; service address; service questionnaire responses; service terms; and related notes.

Quotes, jobs, and media

Quote and invoice numbers, pricing, validity and due dates, terms, electronic signature images, job descriptions, schedules, assignments, completion information, and before-and-after job photos.

Billing and payment information

Invoice and payout records, payment status, currency, amounts, taxes, fees, refund information, card brand and last four digits, card expiry, bank name and last four digits, connected-account status, and identifiers supplied by Stripe. Contractorz does not store complete card or bank account numbers in its application database.

Communications and technical information

Contact-form details and messages, emails sent through the platform, authentication and magic-link tokens, browser-stored login tokens, session and security information, request data, and operational error or server logs generated while providing and securing the service.

3. How we use information

  • Create, verify, authenticate, and maintain accounts.
  • Provide role-based manager, employee, and client workspaces.
  • Connect clients, questionnaires, terms, quotes, jobs, invoices, payments, payouts, and refunds.
  • Send account verification, password reset, questionnaire, quote-signing, invoice, and operational emails.
  • Process contact requests and provide support.
  • Display business information and logos in the public marketplace when the business is active.
  • Protect accounts, enforce permissions, troubleshoot errors, prevent misuse, and maintain the platform.
  • Meet legal, regulatory, accounting, dispute-resolution, and recordkeeping obligations.

4. How we share information

We share information only as needed to provide the platform, follow user instructions, operate integrations, protect the service, or comply with law. Recipients may include:

  • The business, managers, assigned employees, and clients participating in the relevant service workflow, according to their roles and permissions.
  • Stripe for payment methods, connected-account onboarding, charges, payouts, fees, and refunds.
  • Email-delivery providers such as SendGrid for transactional and contact emails.
  • Hosting, storage, database, monitoring, and other technical service providers acting for Contractorz.
  • Professional advisers, regulators, courts, law enforcement, or other parties when disclosure is required or permitted by law.
  • A purchaser or successor in connection with a proposed or completed financing, merger, reorganization, or sale, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections.

Some providers may process information outside Canada, where it may be subject to the laws and lawful access processes of another jurisdiction.

We do not sell personal information or use it for third-party behavioural advertising.

5. Stripe payments

Payment-card entry, payment-method attachment, connected-account onboarding, identity verification, charges, payouts, and refunds are handled through Stripe. Stripe receives information directly and processes it under its own terms and privacy practices.

Contractorz stores limited payment summaries and Stripe identifiers so the platform can show payment, invoice, payout, fee, onboarding, and refund status. Do not enter full card or bank credentials into ordinary text fields, questionnaires, notes, or messages.

6. Retention and deletion

We retain information for as long as reasonably needed to provide accounts and workflows, preserve business and transaction records, resolve disputes, maintain security, and meet legal or contractual obligations.

The platform uses soft deletion for many operational records. Removing a record from an active workspace may therefore hide it without immediately erasing every stored copy. Information may also remain temporarily in backups, logs, email records, or payment-provider systems.

When information is no longer required, we will delete, anonymize, or otherwise securely dispose of it, subject to applicable legal, fraud-prevention, accounting, backup, and dispute requirements.

7. Security and account responsibility

We use administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards appropriate to the nature of the information, including password hashing, authenticated APIs, role and ownership checks, expiring signed links, restricted administrative access, and third-party payment processing.

No system can guarantee absolute security. Users must protect their credentials and devices, avoid sharing authentication or magic-link tokens, assign only appropriate team access, and notify us promptly about suspected unauthorized activity.

8. Your choices and rights

Depending on applicable law, you may ask to access, correct, or receive information about the use and disclosure of your personal information, or withdraw consent where processing is based on consent. Legal and operational exceptions may apply.

Profile information can be reviewed or updated through the account workspace where those controls are available. For information held by a service business about its client, employee, property, or job, contact that business first. Contractorz may assist the business with a request where appropriate.

You may contact us to request account closure or deletion. We will verify the requester and explain any information that must be retained or is controlled by another business or provider.

9. Children

Contractorz is intended for businesses and people participating in service transactions. It is not directed to children, and users must not knowingly submit children's personal information unless they have lawful authority and the information is reasonably necessary for the service.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the platform, providers, or legal requirements change. We will post the revised policy with a new effective date and provide additional notice when required.
